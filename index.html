# the problem
> immune-evasive pathogens mutate constantly — antibodies raised against one variant miss the next
> but their conserved, vulnerable sites don't change — broadly neutralizing antibodies (bNAbs) that target them can recognize many variants at once
> in principle, bNAbs are the solution
> the challenge: these sites are subdominant — bNAbs are rare, and the right immunogens, in what order and dose, are unknown
 
# what we're building
> a virtual immune system (VIS) to design and screen vaccination strategies in silico
> ranking which strategies are most likely to elicit the target bNAbs
> first indication: hepatitis C
> status: stay tuned
