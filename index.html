Vaccine development for immune-evasive pathogens.

first target: hepatitis C
// the problem

Immune-evasive pathogens mutate constantly — antibodies raised against one variant miss the next. Their conserved, vulnerable sites, however, don't change: broadly neutralizing antibodies (bNAbs) against these can recognize many variants at once. In principle, bNAbs are the solution — but these sites are subdominant, so bNAbs are rare, and the right immunogens, in what order and at what dose, remain unknown.

// what we're building

A virtual immune system (VIS) to design and screen vaccination strategies in silico — ranking which are most likely to elicit the target bNAbs. First indication: hepatitis C. Stay tuned.
